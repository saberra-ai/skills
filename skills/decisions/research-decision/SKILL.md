# Research a Decision

When you're not sure of a product or engineering call, **don't wing it and don't just
survey** — research the best practice, cite it, and **recommend**.

## Steps

1. **Frame the real question.** Often it isn't the surface one — "bundle vs fetch" is really
   "size tier × first-run UX × constraints". Name the axes that actually decide it.
2. **Web-search 2–3 targeted queries** — the general best practice *and* how the reference
   products do it (use the current year in the query; name the apps you'd emulate).
3. **Synthesize, don't dump.** Pull the consensus + the explicit trade-off. Tier by
   size/context when the honest answer is "it depends".
4. **Apply the product's filter.** Reconcile with the product's values/constraints and its
   user (e.g. non-technical → outcomes, not implementation).
5. **Recommend.** One recommendation with the reasoning — not a menu. Mark what's the user's
   call vs a sound default you'll take.
6. **Cite.** End with sources as links — the audit trail, and it lets the user verify your read.

## Anti-patterns
- A survey of options with no recommendation.
- Guessing a UX pattern when a reference product has a proven one a search away.
- Research that ignores the product's actual constraints/users.

## Done when
The decision has a cited best-practice basis, a specific recommendation, and a clear split of
"your call" vs "sound default taken".
